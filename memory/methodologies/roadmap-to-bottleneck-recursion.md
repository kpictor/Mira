## Core Idea

`roadmap-to-bottleneck-recursion` starts from a confirmed technology or infrastructure roadmap, then walks upstream layer by layer until it finds the slowest, scarcest, hardest-to-substitute constraint that can plausibly capture economics and map to a public-market proxy.

The method is not "buy every company in the chain." It asks:

1. Which roadmap is already committed by a dominant customer, platform, standard body, regulator or capex owner?
2. What physical, process, material, capacity, certification or integration constraint must be solved for that roadmap to ship?
3. Which upstream node has concentrated supply, long lead time, hard qualification, high switching cost or fragile yield?
4. Can the bottleneck holder actually convert scarcity into revenue, pricing power, margin, backlog, cash flow or strategic value?
5. Is the public-market proxy still mispriced relative to the size and importance of the bottleneck?

## Use When

- A technology, infrastructure or regulatory roadmap is already visible and the investable question is where the next physical or economic constraint sits.
- The first-order opportunity depends on capacity, yield, material purity, equipment lead time, customer qualification, certification, integration complexity or supplier concentration.
- A broad theme risks becoming a generic beneficiary basket unless the chain is recursively narrowed.
- The market may recognize the first bottleneck but not the upstream constraint that enables it.
- The research objective is discovery, watchlist construction or company handoff rather than immediate single-stock conviction.

## Avoid When

- The roadmap itself is not committed or is still marketing-level optionality.
- The bottleneck is mostly consumer taste, brand, content, distribution, policy sentiment or short-term liquidity rather than physical or operational constraint.
- Supply can expand quickly, substitutes are abundant or customers can multi-source without meaningful qualification cost.
- The public company has weak exposure purity and cannot convert bottleneck status into revenue, margin, cash flow or strategic value.
- The theme is already crowded and the method only rationalizes price action after the fact.

## Core Question

Given a committed roadmap, which upstream node is both necessary and slow to replicate, and can that node become an investable public-market proxy before the market fully prices it?

## Required Inputs

- Roadmap source
  Dominant customer/platform disclosure, official product roadmap, standard body material, capex plan, regulatory implementation schedule or credible industry consortium source.
- Chain map
  Downstream system, integration layer, core component, equipment, materials, process, yield/test and logistics nodes.
- Constraint evidence
  Capacity, lead time, yield, qualification cycle, material purity, switching cost, supply concentration, capex intensity and substitute pathway.
- Economic transmission
  How the constraint reaches revenue, ASP, margin, backlog, cash conversion, strategic bargaining power or acquisition value.
- Public-market proxy map
  Tickers, exposure purity, liquidity, coverage, valuation anchor, financial sensitivity and current market label.
- Disconfirmation path
  What would prove the bottleneck is not real, not durable, not company-specific or already priced.

## Primary Signal

The strongest signal is a five-part alignment:

1. `roadmap_confirmed`
   A dominant demand owner has made the route visible and time-bound.
2. `constraint_physical_or_operational`
   The constraint is grounded in physics, process, capacity, certification, integration or material availability.
3. `supply_concentrated_and_slow`
   Only a few suppliers can qualify and expansion takes years or specialized expertise.
4. `economic_transmission_visible`
   The bottleneck can plausibly show up in revenue, pricing, margin, backlog, cash flow or strategic control.
5. `public_market_mismatch`
   The public proxy's market cap, coverage, valuation or narrative label appears mismatched with the node it controls.

## Why It Works

Large technology and infrastructure systems often move in staged constraints. Once the first bottleneck is solved or broadly recognized, capital and attention shift to the next gating layer. A roadmap from a dominant platform can reduce route uncertainty, while upstream recursion can expose which supplier, material or process becomes newly important before ordinary coverage catches up.

The method's edge is not superior technical certainty. The edge is disciplined sequencing:

- start from a route that others have already committed to;
- identify the real gating variable rather than the loudest stock;
- move one layer upstream when the visible layer becomes crowded;
- keep forcing the question of economic transmission.

## Failure Mode

- Treating any value-chain participant as a bottleneck without proving scarcity or qualification difficulty.
- Treating a real technical bottleneck as an investable stock before showing revenue, margin or cash-flow transmission.
- Ignoring substitute architectures that bypass the bottleneck.
- Confusing a company mention, ecosystem partnership or design-in claim with durable value capture.
- Overweighting small market cap mismatch while ignoring dilution, customer concentration, execution history or liquidity.
- Following social-media discovery after the window has already narrowed and crowding has become the main price setter.

## Evidence Cost

medium-high

The first pass can be fast because it starts from roadmap and chain maps. The investable version is more expensive: it needs primary sources, technical validation, company filings, customer evidence, capex/qualification data, and financial transmission checks.

## Comparison To Existing Methods

Relative to `industry-concept-analysis`, this method adds explicit recursion from a roadmap to the next upstream constraint. The existing skill already asks where the bottleneck and profit pool are; this method forces repeated "what enables this layer?" questioning until the chain reaches a slow-to-replicate node.

Relative to `supply-chain` overlay, this method is more directional. Standard supply-chain work maps upstream, downstream and same-layer peers. Roadmap-to-bottleneck recursion starts with a route and searches for the layer most likely to become the next binding constraint.

Relative to `channel-check-scuttlebutt`, this method is not primary research by itself. It identifies which node deserves verification; channel checks can then validate demand, qualification, lead time or customer switching cost.

Relative to `variant-perception`, this method finds the operating or physical source of the variant view. Variant perception still needs to test whether the market has already priced the bottleneck.

## Follow-Through Criteria

- Does it change the bottleneck ranking compared with ordinary industry mapping?
- Does it produce a narrower and better company handoff list?
- Does it identify a concrete disconfirmation metric for each bottleneck candidate?
- Do later filings, earnings calls, capex plans, customer disclosures or pricing data validate the bottleneck?
- Does it avoid false positives where a company is in the chain but lacks value capture?

## Trial Design

### Case 1: CPO / Silicon Photonics

- mode: live trial + forward watch
- baseline: existing `industry-concept-analysis` for CPO plus normal `supply-chain`
- test: start from NVIDIA Quantum-X / Spectrum-X Photonics roadmap, map optical engines, lasers, packaging, fiber attach, testing, foundry processes, materials and upstream inputs.
- expected increment: identify whether the next investable bottleneck is at laser source, InP/materials, TFLN, fiber attach, testing/yield, foundry capacity or another upstream node.
- required output: industry map, recursive bottleneck ladder, company handoff list, evidence log and refresh triggers.

### Case 2: AI Power / Grid Bottleneck

- mode: contrast trial against existing AI power infrastructure work
- baseline: `regional_bottleneck_check` and ordinary AI power value-chain handoff
- test: start from hyperscaler data-center capex and grid-connection constraints, recurse into transformers, switchgear, turbines, interconnection queues, permitting, fuel, water/cooling and regional utility constraints.
- expected increment: prevent a broad AI-power beneficiary basket and improve regional/node-specific company handoff.
- required output: bottleneck ladder, public proxy quality ranking and disconfirmation map.

### Case 3: Historical Failure Backtest

- mode: backtest
- candidates: past themes where a "bottleneck stock" failed to capture economics.
- expected increment: document false-positive patterns such as weak exposure purity, rapid substitution, customer insourcing, financing dilution or no margin capture.

## Falsification Conditions

- It does not change conclusion, watch priority, source-gap map or refresh conditions versus ordinary `industry-concept-analysis`.
- It repeatedly identifies technically interesting nodes that do not transmit into company revenue, margin, backlog, cash flow or strategic value.
- It mostly follows already-crowded social-media stocks and fails to produce earlier or cleaner bottleneck detection.
- Substitute architectures, customer insourcing or multi-sourcing remove the bottleneck faster than expected.
- The method encourages story expansion without evidence logs and source-quality discipline.

## Adoption Decision

Current judgment: `trial`.

Do not patch core `industry-concept-analysis` or `supply-chain` as default behavior yet. Keep this as a trial enhancer until at least two real cases and one follow-through or postmortem prove that it improves bottleneck ranking, company handoff and refresh conditions.
