## Objective

Turn Mira's theory into repeated live case work. The goal is not to add more process text. The goal is to find whether the current workflow can produce institutionally usable research on hot, messy, real-world topics.

## Validation Questions

Each case should answer four workflow questions before being treated as a good Mira sample:

1. Did routing pick the right loop before analysis started?
2. Did evidence classification prevent company claims, forecasts, market pricing and facts from being mixed?
3. Did the quality gate force the conclusion to focus on reality basis, first-order variable, decision increment and disconfirmation path?
4. Did the output get shorter and sharper after review, rather than longer and more procedural?

## Round 1 Acceptance Bar

The workflow is not ready for institutional colleagues until all are true:

- at least three themes have completed real case packages or validation notes
- at least one case is hard-data/infrastructure led, one is event/earnings led, and one is narrative-heavy
- each case has an evidence log or explicit source note trail
- each case records what the workflow improved, what it missed, and what should change
- at least one method is revised, downgraded or rejected based on live evidence
- final public-facing package has no unresolved placeholders and no legacy evidence-log drift

## Current Judgment

Round 1 is an internal validation package with three theme trials and one single-equity handoff sample:

- AI power completed through regional bottleneck mapping and company handoff.
- GLP-1 completed through claim classification and clinical/commercial bridge diagnosis.
- Stablecoins completed through regulatory plumbing and issuer-economics handoff.
- CRCL completed through issuer economics snapshot and scenario/valuation sanity check.

It is not a completed validation. The selected themes are useful because they stress different parts of Mira:

- AI power tests whether Mira can avoid turning a real bottleneck into generic "AI demand" commentary.
- AI capex ROI tests whether Mira can separate business momentum from market expectations and investment payback.
- GLP-1 tests whether Mira can keep clinical/regulatory facts separate from commercial assumptions.
- Physical AI tests whether Mira can downgrade attractive long-duration narratives when evidence is still weak.
- Stablecoins test whether Mira can handle regulatory plumbing without turning it into unsourced fintech enthusiasm.
