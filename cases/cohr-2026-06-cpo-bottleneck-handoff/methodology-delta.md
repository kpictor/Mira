## What The Method Improved

The method prevented a common shortcut:

> CPO is important -> COHR is in optics -> buy COHR.

Instead it forced a more specific bridge:

> NVIDIA AI factory roadmap -> CPO / silicon photonics -> external laser and InP manufacturing bottleneck -> COHR exposure -> revenue/margin/cash-flow transmission -> valuation expectation burden.

This changed the action label from a thematic positive to `watch_only_pending_financial_transmission`.

## Incremental Value

- It identified why COHR is a better first handoff than a generic optical module basket: direct NVIDIA agreement, advanced laser / optical networking product scope, InP and 6-inch platform fit.
- It separated roadmap validation from financial validation.
- It made cash conversion and working capital the gating variable rather than treating revenue growth as sufficient.
- It created a watch dashboard tied to bottleneck transmission: gross margin, cash flow, inventory, AR, CPO/NPO timing and LITE relative quality.

## Remaining Weakness

- This is still not a full single-equity memo.
- No full valuation expectation map has been built.
- The handoff uses market-cap context but does not calculate implied growth, margin or FCF assumptions.
- NVIDIA partnership terms do not disclose exact product volumes, pricing, margins or take-or-pay structure.
- COHR's industrial exposure and capital intensity can dilute the external-laser thesis.

## Trial Decision

Keep `roadmap-to-bottleneck-recursion` under `trial`, but mark this as a constructive second step.

The method improved decision quality because it downgraded the conclusion from "COHR is a CPO winner" to a more falsifiable state:

`watch_only_pending_financial_transmission`
