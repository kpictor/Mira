## Handoff Conclusion

COHR is the best first public-company handoff from the CPO / silicon photonics bottleneck trial, but the correct current label is `watch_only_pending_financial_transmission`, not `upgrade_to_thesis`.

The positive case is strong:

- NVIDIA committed a multiyear agreement, a multibillion-dollar purchase commitment and a $2 billion investment to support COHR's R&D, capacity and U.S. manufacturing.
- COHR's Datacenter & Communications segment is now the company thesis driver, with Q3 FY2026 revenue of $1.362B, about 75% of consolidated revenue.
- COHR's investor presentation directly links next-generation AI scaling to InP wafer production, 6-inch platform expansion, multiple CPO architectures, silicon photonics and advanced optical transport.
- The CPO bottleneck ladder says external lasers / InP laser manufacturing is the first validation path, and COHR sits directly there.

The gating issue is equally important:

- COHR already has a large market-cap rerating.
- FY2026 year-to-date operating cash flow was only $10.1M while CapEx was $547.2M.
- Inventory and accounts receivable rose materially, so the company must prove that capacity build and working capital convert into revenue, margin and cash.
- LITE remains a hard peer benchmark because its Q3 FY2026 growth and margin profile were cleaner in the prior earnings case.

## What The Method Added

Without `roadmap-to-bottleneck-recursion`, COHR would likely be filed as a broad AI optical component winner.

The method sharpened the question:

> Is COHR specifically capturing the external laser / InP manufacturing bottleneck created by CPO and silicon photonics roadmaps, and can that bottleneck show up in margins and cash before expectations outrun reality?

That is a better question than "is AI optical demand strong?"

## Key Evidence

### Roadmap And Customer Validation

The NVIDIA / Coherent agreement is unusually direct. It includes a multiyear strategic agreement, a multibillion-dollar purchase commitment, future access and capacity rights, and a $2B NVIDIA investment in COHR to support R&D, capacity and operations.

Interpretation: this is not proof of future margins, but it is high-quality validation that COHR controls assets NVIDIA wants secured.

### Operating Momentum

COHR Q3 FY2026 revenue was $1.806B, up 20.5% YoY. Datacenter & Communications revenue was $1.362B, up 40.6% YoY and 12.7% QoQ. Non-GAAP gross margin was 39.6%, up 105 bps YoY. Q4 guide was $1.91B-$2.05B of revenue and 39%-41% non-GAAP gross margin.

Interpretation: revenue and margin trend support the handoff.

### Capacity And InP Fit

COHR's Q3 FY2026 presentation states that its 6-inch platform is producing EMLs, CW lasers and photodiodes with higher yields than 3-inch lines, and that multiple CPO architectures, silicon photonics, InP and VCSEL enable flexible networks. The same presentation frames InP wafer production in Texas and NVIDIA-supported U.S. capacity expansion as part of next-generation AI scaling.

Interpretation: this directly matches the bottleneck ladder.

### Financial Quality Gap

The weak point is cash conversion. COHR's FY2026 YTD operating cash flow was $10.1M, while CapEx was $547.2M. Inventory was $2.127B and accounts receivable was $1.188B at Q3 FY2026.

Interpretation: this may be the cost of a major capacity build, but it must reverse or convert. Otherwise the bottleneck story becomes expensive working-capital absorption.

## Expectation Burden

Third-party market data around late May 2026 put COHR's market cap near $74B. That means the market is no longer ignoring the story. The forward question is not whether COHR is relevant to AI optics; it is whether the next 2-4 quarters can justify a much larger equity value with:

- sustained datacenter growth;
- gross margin improvement;
- cash conversion recovery;
- visible NVIDIA / CPO / InP revenue contribution;
- no industrial drag large enough to dilute the thesis.
