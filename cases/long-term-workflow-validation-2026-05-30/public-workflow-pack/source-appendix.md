# Source Appendix

## Source Quality Standard

Use the highest available source for each claim.

Preferred order:

1. SEC / regulator filings
2. company earnings release, 8-K exhibit, annual report or official presentation
3. official transcript or event recording
4. independent industry or macro institution
5. market data provider or estimate aggregator
6. media, expert commentary or analyst interpretation
7. Mira-derived calculation

Each Mira-derived calculation must list upstream sources and formula in the evidence log.

## External Method Sources Used

The methodology was reverse-engineered from public frameworks and practitioner methods, including:

- CFA-style industry and competitive analysis
- Harvard Business School / Porter Five Forces
- McKinsey Consumer Decision Journey
- Jobs-to-Be-Done product framing
- SVPG product discovery risks: value, usability, feasibility, viability
- Mauboussin / value-creation logic: ROIC, reinvestment, competitive advantage period and capital allocation
- Expectations Investing: reverse-engineering price-implied assumptions

Current source gap:

- private buyside process detail remains undercovered
- Chinese-language practitioner search is improved by public Asian/Chinese sources, but remains broad rather than case-level
- public sources provide strong structure but limited direct execution detail

## Public Sharing Rule

Before a case is used externally:

- evidence log must cite stable URLs or local archived source files
- third-party market data must have date and provider
- consensus data must state provider and definition
- derived calculations must include formula and upstream source ids
- no actionability conclusion may depend on a source gap

## Refresh Conditions

- stale_after: 2026-06-30
- must_refresh_if: a source link breaks, a third-party estimate changes materially, a filing supersedes a preliminary release, or a reviewer cannot reproduce a conclusion from the source trail.
