## Why This Comparison Matters

ETN and VRT are both public-market expressions of AI data-center infrastructure, but they stress the workflow differently:

- ETN tests diversified industrial value capture, acquisition-driven exposure, backlog conversion and premium industrial valuation.
- VRT tests high-purity data-center infrastructure exposure, hypergrowth guidance, margin expansion and extreme valuation heat.

If the workflow were weak, it would simply rank VRT above ETN because VRT is the purer theme expression. The trial showed that would be incomplete.

## What The Workflow Got Right

1. It separated theme strength from company value capture.
2. It prevented a broad AI-power bullish conclusion.
3. It differentiated ETN and VRT without forcing a false ranking.
4. It made valuation expectations the blocking item for actionability.
5. It exposed missing fields that a public-grade institutional workflow must include.

## What Still Fails The Public-Grade Bar

- No FY2027/FY2028 consensus estimates.
- No peer/historical valuation range.
- No conference-call transcript extraction.
- No explicit customer concentration or order/backlog breakdown.
- No historical failure backtest.
- No follow-through refresh after Q2 2026.

## Workflow Patch From This Iteration

Add `purity_vs_expectation_burden` block to hot-theme single-equity research:

| field | definition |
| --- | --- |
| `theme_purity_score` | how directly company economics map to the theme |
| `expectation_burden_score` | how much future success current price appears to require |
| `evidence_maturity_score` | how much evidence has moved from narrative to realized financials |
| `valuation_heat_score` | multiple premium and crowding proxy |
| `research_action_implication` | actionability consequence |

Default rule:

- high purity + high burden = watchlist / expectation-map priority, not automatic actionability.
