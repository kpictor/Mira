Without `roadmap-to-bottleneck-recursion`, LITE could be treated as another AI optical winner. The method instead forced a narrower question:

> Is LITE's laser and photonics portfolio directly capturing the external light-source bottleneck created by CPO / silicon photonics, and has the stock already priced that capture?

## Key Evidence

### Roadmap And Customer Validation

The NVIDIA / Lumentum strategic agreement includes a $2B investment and is tied to advanced optics technology, future capacity for advanced laser components and U.S.-based manufacturing expansion.

Interpretation: high-quality customer and roadmap validation.

### Operating Momentum

LITE Q3 FY2026 revenue was $808.4M, up 90.1% YoY. Components revenue was $533.3M, up 77.3% YoY, and Systems revenue was $275.1M, up 121.1% YoY.

Interpretation: LITE is not only a future CPO optionality story; the current optical cycle is already flowing through revenue.

### Margin Transmission

Non-GAAP gross margin reached 47.9% and non-GAAP operating margin reached 32.2%. Management attributed margin expansion to operational rigor, pricing discipline and product mix, including laser chips, pump lasers and narrow linewidth laser assemblies.

Interpretation: this is the strongest evidence that the bottleneck is translating into economics.

### Forward Guide

Lumentum guided Q4 FY2026 revenue to $960M-$1.01B, non-GAAP operating margin to 35%-36% and non-GAAP EPS to $2.85-$3.05.

Interpretation: the near-term revision path is still positive.

### Valuation Burden

StockAnalysis showed LITE at $854.96 on May 29, 2026, with market cap $66.52B, forward PE 53.97 and trailing PE 162.53.

Interpretation: this is now an expectation-management problem. Good operating performance may be necessary but not sufficient.
