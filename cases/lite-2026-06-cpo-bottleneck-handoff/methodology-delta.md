## What The Method Improved

The method prevented a second shortcut:

> LITE has stronger growth and margin than COHR -> LITE is automatically the better stock.

Instead it forced a more useful bridge:

> NVIDIA roadmap -> CPO / OCS / external laser bottleneck -> LITE revenue and margin transmission -> valuation expectation burden.

This produced `watch_only_pending_valuation_map`, not a thesis upgrade.

## Incremental Value

- It confirmed that LITE has cleaner operating transmission than COHR.
- It kept the conclusion from becoming purely relative: cleaner operations do not solve valuation burden.
- It made CPO / OCS timing and margin durability the key refresh variables.
- It strengthened the methodology's ability to downgrade even good-looking bottleneck proxies when the market has already repriced them.

## Remaining Weakness

- No full valuation expectation map has been built.
- No company-level cash-flow statement from the latest 10-Q was incorporated into this handoff.
- NVIDIA partnership terms do not disclose exact volumes, pricing, margins or take-or-pay structure.
- Management language around future CPO / OCS earnings power is still forward-looking.
- Current evidence does not quantify what share of LITE revenue is directly tied to CPO external light sources.

## Trial Decision

Keep `roadmap-to-bottleneck-recursion` under `trial`.

The method now has:

- one industry-level CPO trial;
- one COHR company handoff;
- one LITE company contrast handoff.

This is constructive, but still short of adoption because there is no follow-through refresh, no historical failure backtest and no valuation map proving decision improvement.
