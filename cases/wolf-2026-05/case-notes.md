## Intermediate Notes

### Business And Industry

Wolfspeed is a SiC materials and power device company. The old EV-centered SiC cycle was not enough to support the capital structure, leading to Chapter 11 in 2025. The new narrative is broader: AI data center power density, grid modernization, industrial electrification, aerospace/defense, and 300mm SiC for future advanced packaging.

Competitive advantage is plausible but not yet proven in financials. Confirmed strengths include SiC specialization, vertically integrated U.S. supply chain, 200mm footprint, 10kV MOSFET launch, and 300mm wafer milestone. The missing evidence is commercial: named customers, purchase commitments, design-win conversion and gross margin repair.

### Financial Quality

Financial quality is a survival gate, not the investment anchor.

Q3 FY2026:

- revenue: $150.2M
- GAAP gross margin: (27)%
- non-GAAP gross margin: (21)%
- GAAP net loss: ($119.9M)
- adjusted EBITDA: ($61.7M)
- operating cash flow: ($83.8M)
- liquidity: $1.1648B cash, cash equivalents and short-term investments
- Q4 FY2026 guide: $140M-$160M revenue and negative gross margins

Balance sheet is improved vs pre-restructuring, but the operating model is not yet fixed. The March 2026 refinancing lowers annual interest expense by about $62M and total debt by about $97M, which improves runway and equity optionality. Dilution remains material because current shares exclude future conversions, warrants and incentive plans.

### Macro And Financing Environment

Macro is favorable for the new WOLF story. IEA and EIA both support the broad premise that AI data centers are stressing power systems and driving electricity demand. This makes SiC power conversion more relevant than it looked under a pure EV-cycle frame.

Financing environment matters because WOLF remains cash-burning. The March 2026 private placement was an important positive signal: equity/warrant issuance at a premium and participation by T. Rowe Price/Fidelity-advised accounts suggest the financing window is open for now.
