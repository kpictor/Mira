## Core Conclusion

The humanoid robotics theme is real enough to study but not clean enough for a direct single-equity conclusion.

The workflow should block a broad "buy humanoid robotics" conclusion because the public-market handoff is weak:

- the highest-purity humanoid OEMs are often private
- public companies with direct narratives have little disclosed humanoid revenue
- public companies with real robotics revenue are often industrial, warehouse or automation businesses rather than humanoid pure plays
- enabling platforms may win, but the humanoid revenue bridge is not separated from broader AI/platform revenue

The correct research action is:

`industry_map_first`

## Facts

- NVIDIA has official physical-AI / Isaac / GR00T platform announcements and robotics ecosystem partnerships.
- Tesla investor materials include Optimus capacity under construction, but do not provide segment revenue, external customer deployments or unit economics.
- Teradyne has a commercial Robotics segment, but it is collaborative/mobile industrial robotics rather than humanoid pure play.
- Symbotic has commercial warehouse automation revenue and large backlog, but is not a humanoid robotics pure play.

## Inferences

- The most investable public exposure today may be enabling infrastructure and automation adjacencies, not direct humanoid OEMs.
- Product demos and ecosystem announcements are not the same as paid deployments with margin evidence.
- The workflow's handoff gate is doing useful work by preventing a hot-theme shortcut.

## Judgments

No single public company in this screen merits direct long-term humanoid robotics actionability based only on theme exposure.

Candidate research paths:

1. `NVDA`: platform/enabler case, but only if robotics revenue bridge can be separated from general AI infrastructure.
2. `TER`: commercial robotics segment-quality case, not a humanoid thesis.
3. `SYM`: warehouse automation backlog-quality case, not a humanoid thesis.
4. `TSLA`: high-optionality robot OEM case, but blocked until production, customer, revenue and unit-economics evidence improves.

## Stop Rules

- If there is no material segment revenue or backlog tied to humanoid robotics, do not issue actionability.
- If the public expression is only an enabling layer, describe it as an enabler thesis, not a humanoid pure-play.
- If the company is already valued on another dominant business, do not smuggle humanoid optionality into the core valuation without an expectation map.
