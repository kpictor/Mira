## Core Conclusion

CRM is the first product-led trial where the workflow changes the question. For ETN and VRT, the blocking issue was valuation burden against strong infrastructure demand. For CRM, valuation is not the primary obstacle. The obstacle is proof that Agentforce and Data 360 can move from product traction to total-company acceleration.

Salesforce disclosed stronger-than-narrative product evidence: $1.2B Agentforce ARR, nearly $3.4B Agentforce and Data 360 ARR, 3.8B Agentic Work Units, premium SKU bookings growth, token processing, Data 360 record ingestion and Slack MCP active users. That gets CRM beyond `concept`, `demo` and likely `pilot`.

But the workflow still blocks a strong long-term conclusion. FY27 revenue guidance implies about 11% growth including about 3 points from Informatica contribution. Until Agentforce/Data 360 metrics are tied to organic revenue acceleration, retention, pricing and margin conversion, the right action is `watch_only_pending_product_monetization_map`.

## Bull Case

The bull case is that Salesforce has a real agentic AI product platform inside a large installed base. Agentforce and Data 360 already show paid ARR and usage evidence, while more than 50% of related bookings came from existing customers in Q1. If AI agents become a native workflow layer across sales, service, Slack, data and integration products, Salesforce could reaccelerate organic growth without giving up margins.

Valuation is not excessive compared with the AI infrastructure cases. On current price and company FY27 non-GAAP EPS guidance midpoint, CRM trades at roughly 13.6x non-GAAP EPS guidance.

## Bear Case

The bear case is that Agentforce metrics are real but not yet company-moving. Usage metrics such as Agentic Work Units, tokens and records ingested can grow quickly without proving durable enterprise willingness to pay, retention, pricing power or margin conversion.

The FY27 guide still includes about 3 points from Informatica contribution, and the company says organic revenue acceleration is expected in the second half. That acceleration is not yet proven. The large ASR and debt issuance also complicate EPS, share count and capital allocation interpretation.

## Key Debate

The key debate is not whether Salesforce has AI products. It does.

The key debate is whether those products are:

- standalone product traction,
- packaging / cross-sell inside the existing installed base,
- or a durable platform shift that changes total-company organic growth, retention and margins.

## Product Evidence Ladder

CRM reaches:

- `paid_deployment`: Agentforce ARR disclosed at $1.2B.
- `repeat_usage`: Agentic Work Units and token volume disclosed.
- `expansion`: existing-customer bookings and premium SKU bookings disclosed.

CRM has not yet proven:

- `retention`: no Agentforce-specific retention/churn metric.
- `pricing_power`: premium SKU growth exists, but price realization is not separately disclosed.
- `margin_conversion`: company margin is strong, but product-level margin contribution and AI cost curve are not disclosed.

## Actionability Bridge

research_action: `watch_only_pending_product_monetization_map`

## Evidence Quality

Evidence quality is high for revenue, cRPO, EPS, operating margin, free cash flow and guidance because these come from SEC-furnished materials. Evidence quality is medium for product traction: Salesforce discloses product ARR and usage metrics, but the linkage to retention, pricing, organic acceleration and product-level margin remains incomplete.

## Framework Mismatch Risk

The main mismatch risk is treating AI product metrics like infrastructure backlog. In software, usage and ARR can be bundled, repriced or cross-sold through the existing base. The workflow must ask whether product evidence changes net retention and durable growth, not just whether usage is large.
