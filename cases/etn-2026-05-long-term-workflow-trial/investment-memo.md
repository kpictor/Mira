## Core Conclusion

ETN passes the `theme_to_company_handoff` gate. Eaton is not merely adjacent to AI power demand; it has a direct supplier/enabler role through electrical power management, data-center systems and Boyd Thermal's thermal components/systems exposure.

But the workflow prevents a blanket bullish conclusion. The evidence supports a credible long-term operating thesis, while the valuation bridge shows that much of the quality and growth story may already be embedded in the price. At the 2026-05-29 close of $400.82, ETN traded at about 30.2x the midpoint of FY2026 adjusted EPS guidance and about 36.1x the midpoint of FY2026 GAAP EPS guidance. Without a consensus/peer/historical expectation map, the correct research action is `watch_only_pending_expectation_map`.

## Bull Case

The bull case is that data-center power demand is not a one-year pull-forward but a multi-year grid-to-rack infrastructure cycle. In that scenario, Eaton's Electrical Americas backlog, Electrical Global demand, Boyd Thermal acquisition and power-management portfolio convert into sustained organic growth, high segment margins and high-return capital deployment.

The strongest evidence today is official: Q1 2026 sales grew 17%, organic sales grew 10%, Electrical Americas sales grew 20%, Electrical Americas rolling twelve-month orders grew 42% organically, and Electrical Americas backlog grew 44% year over year.

## Bear Case

The bear case is valuation and duration. If AI capex growth normalizes, data-center deployments slip because of power/permitting constraints, customers defer orders, or acquisitions dilute margins, ETN can still be a high-quality company while the stock underperforms.

Electrical Americas operating margin was 25.6% in Q1 2026 versus 30.0% in Q1 2025. That does not disprove the thesis, but it makes margin durability a live issue. The Boyd Thermal acquisition also improves strategic exposure while increasing integration and capital-allocation risk.

## Key Debate

The key debate is not whether Eaton has AI-power exposure. It does.

The real debate is whether current valuation already capitalizes the next several years of AI/data-center electrical infrastructure growth, leaving too little room for execution error.

## Framework Mismatch Risk

The main mismatch risk is treating ETN like a pure AI infrastructure stock. Eaton is diversified, acquisition-active and still exposed to non-data-center industrial, aerospace and mobility dynamics. A pure-theme framework would overstate purity; a generic industrial framework would understate AI power optionality.
