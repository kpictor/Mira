## Core Conclusion

The defense autonomy and drone theme is real enough to study, but a broad "buy drone/autonomy defense winners" conclusion should be blocked.

The workflow routes the theme to `industry_map_first_with_single_equity_candidates` because public-company value capture differs by node:

- AVAV is a high-purity public candidate after BlueHalo, but needs funded backlog, acquisition value-capture and margin work
- KTOS has unmanned exposure, but needs program and margin evidence
- primes may benefit, but exposure is diluted
- PLTR and defense software names need program-level monetization evidence
- private companies like Anduril may be among the cleanest expressions, limiting public-market purity

The right next step is not a generic actionability call. It is a node map plus selected single-equity cases with procurement/program and expectation-map gates.

## Facts

- DoD announced Replicator capabilities as part of a fielding priority for attritable autonomous systems.
- DIU public materials present Replicator as a way to field autonomous systems through commercial and non-traditional channels.
- DoD budget and counter-UAS source paths are required to distinguish policy priorities from funded procurement.
- AeroVironment and BlueHalo completed a transaction combining unmanned systems, loitering munitions, space and counter-UAS capabilities.
- AVAV, KTOS and PLTR filing paths are required to verify backlog, revenue mix, margin, contract type and program economics.

## Inferences

- Defense demand is credible, but government demand does not equal public-company equity value capture.
- Rapid fielding and prototype pathways can create real revenue but may not have the durability of a program of record.
- High-purity private defense-technology companies reduce the purity of public-market expressions.
- Acquisition-driven exposure can raise theme purity while adding purchase-price, integration and margin risk.

## Judgments

The workflow should add a new overlay:

`government-procurement-program-check.csv`

Reason:

The current long-term method covers industry, execution and valuation, but defense procurement requires a harder gate around funded budget, contract status, program-of-record path, production delivery, compliance, export constraints and margin risk.

## Candidate Follow-Up Cases

1. `AVAV`: likely best public single-equity candidate. Required gates: expectation map, BlueHalo acquisition value-capture, funded backlog, contract quality, program status and working-capital/margin risk.
2. `KTOS`: unmanned systems candidate. Required gates: program status, funded backlog, margin quality, tactical drone exposure and valuation expectations.
3. `PLTR`: defense AI/software layer. Required gates: program-level monetization, renewal/expansion, classified/government compliance and valuation burden.
4. Large primes: segment-purity candidates only if autonomy/counter-UAS program economics become material.

## Stop Rules

- Do not treat DoD priority language as revenue unless funding and contract status are evidenced.
- Do not treat a prototype, OTA or CSO pathway as durable value capture without delivery and follow-on program evidence.
- Do not treat acquisition-driven exposure as organic capability without purchase-price, integration and ROIC path.
- Do not treat defense software wins as autonomy value capture without program-level revenue and renewal evidence.
- Do not use national-security urgency as a valuation conclusion without an expectation map.

## Ordinary Vs Workflow Delta

Ordinary likely conclusion:

- Defense autonomy, drones and counter-UAS are urgent priorities; buy public drone/autonomy beneficiaries.

Workflow conclusion:

- Broad theme remains `industry_map_first_with_single_equity_candidates`.
- AVAV and KTOS may deserve single-equity research first.
- PLTR and primes require program-level monetization and segment-purity work.
- Private defense-tech purity limits public-market handoff.

Useful delta:

- The workflow separates policy urgency from funded program economics.
- It adds a procurement/program overlay that was missing from the public workflow pack.
- It blocks a high-heat defense theme from becoming a generic beneficiary basket.
