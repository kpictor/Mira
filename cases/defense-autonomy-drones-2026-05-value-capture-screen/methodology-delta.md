## Why The Change Is Needed

Defense autonomy themes can look obvious at the policy level. The hard question is whether a public company captures durable equity economics after:

- appropriated funding
- contract status
- program-of-record path
- production and delivery
- compliance/security requirements
- export or allied-sales constraints
- acquisition integration
- unit economics and margin risk
- valuation expectations

These variables are specific enough that they should not be buried inside generic industry or company execution analysis.

## Methodology Judgment

Keep the change.

This overlay is likely useful for:

- defense autonomy and drone cases
- counter-UAS themes
- national-security software cases
- federally funded infrastructure and industrial policy cases
- any theme where policy priority must convert into funded contracts

It should remain a triggered overlay, not a default requirement for every long-term thesis.
